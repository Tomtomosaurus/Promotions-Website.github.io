From when I was very little, I had a passion for PCs, or more accurately, PC video games.

This was fueled by my obsessive watching of YouTubers who played these various games, such as Kwebblekop, Jelly, Slogoman, Ssundee, DanTDM, Mumbo Jumbo, McCreamy and Lazarbeam.

As you may probably have noticed, these were primarily Minecraft and Fortnite YouTubers (yes, I was that type of kid)

But it wasn't just gamers that I watched. I also focused on engineers, chemists and doctors, whose videos were themed around informing and entertaining people at the same time.

These YouRubers were such as Peter Stripol, Mark Rober, Nile Red, Veritasium, Kevin Talbot, Raz Shifrin, Styropyro, and many more.

Ultimately, this all led me to discover certain tech YouTubers, the most prominent being MKBHD, Gamer's Nexus, Austin Evans, and, most importantly, Linus Tech Tips.

It was really mostly Linus that gave me my interest in computers. He taught me how to build them and how fun it is to work with them.

Eventually, I was coaxed into saving up for and building my own computer.

It turned out amazing, with lots of RGB and enough power to last me for years to come. I built it around late November - early December of 2021, during the height of the GPU crisis.
